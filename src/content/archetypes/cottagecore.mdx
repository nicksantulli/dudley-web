<p class="tldr">Cottagecore is an aesthetic that romanticizes a simple, slow rural life — gardens, baking, wildflowers, linen, and harmony with nature.</p>

## What is Cottagecore? 🌿

Cottagecore took off online as a gentle counter-aesthetic to fast, urban, screen-filled life. It imagines a softer existence: tending a garden, baking bread, picking wildflowers, mending clothes by hand, and living in rhythm with the seasons. It's nostalgia for a countryside that's part real, part fairytale.

## What Cottagecore actually means

At heart, cottagecore is about slowness, self-sufficiency, and finding beauty in ordinary domestic things. The palette is warm and natural — cream, sage, dusty rose — and the textures are soft: linen, cotton, knitwear, dried flowers. But the aesthetic is really a value system: care over speed, handmade over mass-produced, enough over more.

From the outside it looks like a children's-book illustration come to life. From the inside it feels like permission to rest, make things slowly, and let the day be quiet.

## Signs you might be Cottagecore

- You've fantasized about owning a little cottage with a vegetable garden
- Baking, jam-making, or growing herbs genuinely relaxes you
- Your wardrobe leans toward linen, florals, and soft knits
- You'd rather be barefoot in grass than anywhere loud
- You collect small natural things — pressed flowers, pretty stones, jars
- "Slow morning" is your favorite kind of morning
