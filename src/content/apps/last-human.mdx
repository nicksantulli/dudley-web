## What is Last Human: Dodge the Bots?

Last Human: Dodge the Bots is a free satirical arcade game for iPhone. You're the last human employee in an AI-automated office — dodge the AutoBots advancing toward your desk, grab power-ups from the break room, and reach the time clock before your shift ends and your position gets deprecated.

The memo screen between each level is a full-screen corporate memo from AutoBot 9000 explaining, with complete sincerity, why the bots now handle your former colleagues' responsibilities. That memo is the punchline. And the ad break.

Last Human is free on the App Store with no in-app purchases. Ads are non-personalized — no tracking, no tracking prompt.

## How Last Human works

1. **Navigate the office floor.** Swipe in four directions across the top-down office grid — cubicles, break room, printer station, conference room. Environmental obstacles block movement; hazards move through the space.
2. **Dodge the bots.** AutoBots advance methodically from every direction, each one heading for your desk. A bot contact costs a life. You have three lives per level.
3. **Grab power-ups.** A Coffee Mug gives you a speed burst. A Stress Ball makes you briefly invincible. A USB Drive ("Backup Plan") adds an extra life. None of them stops the bots; they just buy you time.
4. **Reach the time clock.** The win condition is in the bottom-right of the floor plan. Every level is a race between you and the clock — and the bots keep coming.
5. **Read the memo.** Between each level, a full-screen corporate memo appears from HR-Bot 3.0 explaining the latest "workforce optimization update." Tap to continue. The interstitial fires over the memo screen. That is the joke.

## The office hazards

**Bot types:** AutoBot Standard advances in a straight patrol line. AutoBot Enhanced detects you at range and gives chase. Meeting Bot bounces erratically — unpredictable. HR Bot holds a fixed position but expands its detection zone periodically. New bots are introduced as levels escalate.

**Environmental hazards:** Flying Memos fire across the floor in straight paths from the printer station. Rogue Roombas bounce off walls randomly. Cascading Printer Paper briefly zones out an area. An Empty Coffee Cup slows your movement for 1.5 seconds.

**Difficulty curve:** Levels 1–2 are AutoBots only — tutorial feel. By levels 7–10, all four bot types appear simultaneously with environmental hazards at accelerated speed. There is no difficulty selector in v1. The only way out is through.

## Why Last Human?

The AI automation story is a lot easier to deal with when there's a time clock and a Rogue Roomba. Last Human doesn't pretend the bots are a sci-fi threat — they're blinking terminal screens on wheels, and the villain is the memo, not the machine. The satirical target is the institution and its jargon, not anyone who works in one.

No real companies, no real AI systems, no real people appear in the game. The bots are AutoBot 9000 and friends: entirely fictional, entirely smug.
